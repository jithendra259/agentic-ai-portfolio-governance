SECTION 1: MATHEMATICAL FORMALISM OF G-CVaR

1.1 THE OBJECTIVE FUNCTION
The Graph-Augmented Conditional Value at Risk (G-CVaR) objective is defined as:

    min J(w) = CVaR_alpha(w) + Lambda_t * G(w, c_t)

Where:
- w: Vector of portfolio weights (n x 1)
- alpha: Confidence level (typically 0.95 or 0.99)
- Lambda_t: Time-varying adaptive trust coefficient.
- G(w, c_t): Linear contagion penalty term, c_t^T * w.
- c_t: Eigenvector centrality vector derived from the institutional holdings graph.

1.2 LINEARIZATION OF CVAR
To ensure convexity and computational efficiency, we utilize the Rockafellar-Uryasev (2000) auxiliary function F_alpha(w, zeta):

    F_alpha(w, zeta) = zeta + (1 / (1-alpha)) * E[ max( -R^T * w - zeta, 0 ) ]

By substituting the expectation with a sample mean over T trading days:

    min J(w, zeta, z) = zeta + (1 / (T * (1-alpha))) * sum(z_t) + Lambda_t * sum(c_i * w_i)

Subject to:
    z_t >= -r_t^T * w - zeta    (for all t in 1...T)
    z_t >= 0                    (slack variables)
    sum(w) = 1                  (budget constraint)
    w_i >= 0                    (long-only)
    w_i <= 0.15                 (position limit)

1.3 SPECTRAL PROPERTIES OF THE CONTGON NETWORK
The Institutional Network is a bipartite graph G = (V_s, V_i, E).
The projection onto the Stock space (V_s) creates a weighted adjacency matrix A_s.
The penalty term utilizes Eigenvector Centrality because it captures "Iterative Vulnerability." 
A stock is high-centrality if it is held by institutions who themselves hold many 
other high-centrality stocks. This represents the "fire-sale" epicenter.

SECTION 2: THE COMPOSITE INSTABILITY INDEX (I_t)

2.1 HARDENED INSTABILITY FORMULA
The Instability Index I_t is the "thermostat" of the system. In the production hardening phase (Phase 11), the formula was stabilized to:

    I_t = 0.40 * Norm(Volatility) + 0.30 * Norm(Correlation) + 0.30 * Norm(Max_Drawdown)

- Volatility (40%): Computed as the annualized 21-day rolling standard deviation within the 252-day window.
- Correlation (30%): The mean pairwise Pearson correlation of all constituents in the universe. Rising correlations are a primary signal of systemic collapse ("Correlations go to 1.0 in a crisis").
- Max Drawdown (30%): The maximum peak-to-trough decline of the universe's equal-weight benchmark within the window.

2.2 THE SIGMOID ADAPTIVE TRUST FUNCTION
Lambda_t is mapped from I_t using a Sigmoid function:

    Lambda_t = L_max / ( 1 + exp( -k * (I_t - I_thresh) ) )

Default Hyperparameters:
- L_max = 1.0 (Maximum penalty intensity)
- k = 10 (Steepness of activation)
- I_thresh = 0.85 (The "Crisis Event" floor)

This creates the "Dormancy Property": when markets are calm (I_t < 0.5), Lambda_t is effectively zero, and the system behaves like a standard CVaR optimizer. When a crash is detected (I_t > 0.85), Lambda_t surges, forcing the portfolio out of systemically central stocks.

SECTION 3: BACKTESTING AND VALIDATION

3.1 UNIVERSE DEFINITIONS (U1 - U11)
The system is validated across 11 Sector-Universe groupings (U1-U11), including Technology (U1), Energy (U5), and Financials (U6). Each universe contains the top-50 most liquid tickers by market cap as of the benchmark date.

3.2 WALK-FORWARD PERFORMANCE
The walk-forward analysis (51 windows, 100-day steps) confirms that G-CVaR reduces Max Drawdown by 15-22% across crisis regimes (2008, 2020) compared to equal-weight and standard mean-variance baselines.

- In Calm regimes, G-CVaR tracks Standard CVaR with a tracking error of < 0.05%.
- In Crisis regimes, G-CVaR shifts exposure toward "Peripheral" stocks (low institutional overlap), significantly reducing fire-sale contagion risk.
